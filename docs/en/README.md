# Nayota Yoga 

Welcome to the **Nayota Yoga** digital resource space.

If you wish to understand the original intention behind creating this resource space and the story behind "Nayota" and "Wuchang" (Impermanence), please read the [Preface & About Me](en/preface.md).

This website is dedicated to organizing and sharing the theoretical content, practical sequences, and core summaries of a 10-week Vinyasa Flow Yoga course. I hope to plant a seed for independent yoga practice in everyone who visits here. Our ultimate goal is: to practice regularly and quietly, guided solely by the physical and mental feedback of our own bodies, without the need for external verbal guidance. To gradually feel the changes in our physical space until we can observe and expand our inner space.

## 📚 Summer Semester Course Structure
**Nayota Yoga 10-Week University Sports Course**

The first part focuses on the practice of foundational asanas (poses). In each session, we will focus on experiencing one foundational asana, and through offline flow sequences (Vinyasa), we will feel where our awareness should be directed while practicing these asanas. Through deliberate practice, this will become a habit, and ideally, a completely natural physiological response.

1. **What** the asana actually looks like: A standard illustration.
   
2. The **Philosophy Behind** the asana: Since "standard" poses are external averages and our bodies are entirely unique, the goal is to adapt the asana for your own body rather than forcing yourself into a template. The fundamental benchmark is whether you can maintain smooth and steady breathing. Yoga is an experiential process rather than a race for results. While we keep a target alignment in mind, the journey from our present physical state to that goal is what truly matters. *(For more details on breathing, extension, and creating space, please refer to the [Philosophy](en/philosophy.md) section).*
   
3. **Practical Sequences**: Practical sequences designed to help you physically feel this underlying philosophy and continuously gain more space.
