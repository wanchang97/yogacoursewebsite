# Week 2: Grounding - Tabletop Details & Flow

**Introduction**: We try to experience building a foundation connected to the earth from our palms and soles through simple Tabletop and Downward-Facing Dog poses. Starting from Mountain Pose, we feel how to transmit this foundation to the whole body, experiencing the support, stability, and space of our organs.

---

## A. Theory & Anatomy

### 1. Establishing Palm Support, Activating the Inner Upper Arms
The webbing between the thumb and index finger, and the roots of all five fingers connect with the ground. The upper arms slightly rotate outwards, activating the inner upper arms, which effortlessly transmits strength from the shoulder blades to the palms and down to the ground.

![Palm Support](../figures/Theory/palm.png)

- Start feeling the palm support from the Tabletop Pose.
- Lift one hand or foot in Tabletop Pose, feel the support of the remaining palms or soles, and the stability and extension of other body parts.
- Transition from Tabletop Pose to Downward-Facing Dog in the second class, feeling the palm support and the building of arm strength.
- Experience One-Legged Downward Dog in the second class, feeling the palm support and the building of arm strength.


### 2. Establishing Foot Support, Activating the Legs
The triangle formed by the heel, the root of the little toe, and the root of the big toe roots firmly into the ground. When standing, feel the full activation of the inner, outer, and back of the thighs (especially the often-neglected inner thighs).

![Foot Triangle](../figures/Theory/feetTriangle.jpg)

- Start finding a sense of stability from Mountain Pose standing, find the feeling of shifting the center of gravity forward, backward, left, and right.
- Transition from Mountain Pose to Warrior I, feeling the foot support and the activation of the inner thighs.
- Transition from Warrior I to Warrior II, feeling the foot support and the activation of the inner thighs.
- Transition from Warrior II to Triangle Pose, feeling the foot support and the activation of the inner thighs.
