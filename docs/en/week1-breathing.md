# Week 1: Breathing & Space

**Introduction**: Yoga is not just about asanas (postures); it is a philosophy and art of living. The essence of yoga is to control the fluctuations of the mind. Vinyasa yoga is the integration of posture and breath. 
This week, we do not pursue complex movements. We simply learn how to "sit well" and attempt to connect our consciousness and body through breathing, experiencing the space within our bodies in simple postures.

---

## A. Theory

### 1. Breathing
#### 1.1 Purely Experiencing the Breath
Breathing is something everyone does every moment. We can start to understand ourselves by observing our breath.
Sit on the yoga mat. You can sit in Easy Pose (Sukhasana), Thunderbolt Pose (Vajrasana), or casually cross-legged. If you feel unstable, try elevating your hips with a block or cushion, so you can better feel the internal rise and fall with your breath.

* **Lateral Expansion Practice**: Place your hands on your abdomen and ribs. Feel your abdomen and ribs expanding outward as you inhale, and contracting inward as you exhale, while the back gently rounds and stretches. You can also try the "Who practice": Inhale through the nose and exhale through the mouth, slowly blowing out like blowing a candle, making a long, soft "who" sound like the wind.
* **Vertical Extension Practice**: As you inhale, feel your body growing taller upwards, the space in your chest and abdomen expanding upwards with the incoming air. At this time, the hand on your stomach should feel a slight inward descent rather than outward expansion. Exhale slowly through the nose, relaxing and stabilizing the body.

#### 1.2 Breathing in Poses: Feel Extension on Inhale, Stability on Exhale
* **Inhale**: Feel the body extending upwards and forwards.
* **Exhale**: Feel the body rooting downwards, gaining stability and relaxation as deep air is expelled.

During every pose, you can start feeling from your internal organs. Draw the breath deep into your abdomen, bring it to every internal organ, and feel the space inside your body. Empty the air as you exhale, feeling your heart, lungs, stomach, intestines, etc., being stably supported.

### 2. Awareness of Extension
The first intention of our stretching sensation is our organs (chest, abdomen). The second intention is our entire torso and limbs.

**A note on "Gravity and Resistance":**
*Quoted from Anatomy of Movement — Blandine Calais-Germain*  
*[Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Anatomy+of+Movement+Blandine+Calais-Germain) | [Author's Official Website](https://www.calais-germain.com/)*
Many movements of the body are completed not only by muscle contraction but by gravity. 
For example, during a side bend, it is gravity that guides you downwards. At this time, the muscles on the outer side of the body are **eccentrically contracting while being lengthened**, resisting gravity to let the body descend slowly. This is why yoga focuses on the process: the slow descent is the process of muscles lengthening to resist gravity.
For example, in Uttanasana, the even and slow lengthening of the muscles at the back of the body allows us to descend slowly instead of falling out of control instantly.

## C. Teacher's Script
*(Teacher's Instruction Area: Three Rounds of Flow)*

### Round 1: Detailed Breakdown (Focus: Connecting Awareness and Breath)
* **Goal**: Teach students how to "bring the breath into the movement" rather than just doing stretching gymnastics.
* **Cue Focus**: When pausing in each movement (like seated side bend), constantly remind them to "inhale and extend the spine upwards, exhale and let the shoulders relax downwards." Tell them not to strive for how far their hands can reach, but to feel the spaces between the ribs being opened by the breath.

### Round 2: Flow with Breath and Music (Focus: Smoothness and Focus)
* **Goal**: Reduce anatomical jargon, let students get into the Flow.
* **Cue Focus**: "Inhale look up, exhale look down", "Inhale to the left, exhale to center". Give only movement instructions.

### Round 3: Self-Exploration Flow (Focus: Introspection)
* **Goal**: Close eyes, let students do it again at their own breathing pace.
* **Advanced Options**: Try Reverse Prayer for forward folds, try transitioning from cross-legged to Half Lotus seated forward fold.
